## Day 009 — Lesson A (Foundations) review notes

### Key ideas to check

- You can independently design and implement small helpers.
- You are comfortable mixing strings, numbers, arrays, and simple objects.
- You are writing at least minimal checks for your functions.

### Discussion points

- Which helper felt easiest? Which felt trickiest? Why?
- Did any of your initial implementations fail your own tests?
- How confident do you feel about spotting edge cases now compared to Day 001?

### Common improvements

- Keep function names and messages descriptive.
- Avoid reintroducing patterns you already improved earlier in the week
  (e.g., mutating inputs, mixing logging with logic).
- When in doubt, add one more tiny test rather than guessing.

### Stretch questions

- If you had to explain what you learned in Week 1 to another developer,
  which 3–4 key ideas would you highlight?
